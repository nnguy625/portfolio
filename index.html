Case Study

The Character Consistency
Problem

AI image generation can't hold a character's appearance consistent from one generation to the next — ask for the same person twice and you get two different faces. That instability is the core technical challenge in AI comic and animation production. Generating one good image is easy; maintaining a single, recognizable character across a full narrative is the hard problem.

The Pipeline

The Proof

Secondary proof — consistency across pose and lighting. The same character rendered across multiple shots from the trained LoRA — three-quarter, front, cinematic, and full-body — holding consistent face, build, and design as pose, framing, and lighting change.

The insight: Stacking multiple conditioning systems — FaceID + character LoRA + ControlNet — created competing signals that actually degraded consistency instead of reinforcing it. Simplifying to clean, LoRA-driven generation produced cleaner, more consistent output. The real lesson is production-first judgment: build for what holds up in production, not for what demos well.
